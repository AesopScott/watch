# ADR 0001 — Subscription Pricing Tier

**Status:** Accepted  
**Date:** 2026-05-22

## Context
The Soul doc targets $5K MRR and 500 paying subscribers. Dividing these produces $10/month — below market rate for this audience. Comparable advanced developer platforms (Frontend Masters $39/month, Epic React $30/month, exclusive indie communities $50–$150/month) show this audience pays premium prices for premium access.

## Decision
Target $100/month for subscriber pricing.

## Rationale
- $100/month requires ~50 subscribers to hit $5K MRR (vs. 500 at $10/month)
- 50 subscribers is a very achievable 6-month goal for a new premium platform
- $100/month anchors WATCH firmly as elite access, not a casual subscription
- Consistent with "Not Basic Education" identity — this is for people who take their AI career seriously enough to invest $1,200/year
- Underpricing attracts price-sensitive users who expect hand-holding — exactly the wrong audience

## Consequences
- Soul goals updated: target is 15–20 subscribers ($1,500–$2,000 MRR). 500 was wrong at this price point.
- Marketing must justify the price (social proof, preview content, clear ROI for the subscriber)
- Cannot easily raise price later once subscribers are anchored — lock in at $100 early
